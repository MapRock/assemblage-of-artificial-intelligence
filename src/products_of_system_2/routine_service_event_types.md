# Routine Service Delivery Event Types (Embedding-Optimized Descriptions)

The events of a routine service delivery, generally requiring trained service providers. For examples of routine service delivery:

- Serving a guest at a full-service restaurant.
- Assisting a customer during grocery shopping and checkout.
- Lawn service making their weekly stop to mow the lawn.
- Primary care doctor conducting an annual wellness checkup.
- Regular home cleaning service performing a scheduled visit.

Events are presented in the typical **average** ordinal in which they occur. That means they can occur in any order and can looped back upon.

## Events

### 1.0 arrival_greeting

The initial contact or welcome when the service provider first interacts with the customer/guest/patient upon arrival or start of service. This includes acknowledgment, confirmation of appointment or identity, and any immediate setup of rapport. Typical language includes phrases like “welcome”, “good to see you”, “how are you today”, “I’m here for your appointment”, “party of”, or “ready to get started”.

### 2.0 needs_assessment

Gathering of the customer’s current needs, preferences, expectations, or any updates since last interaction. This step captures what the customer wants from the service today. Typical language includes “what can I get for you”, “how are things going”, “any concerns today”, “what would you like”, “tell me about your symptoms”, or “any special requests”.

### 3.0 preparation_setup

Actions taken to ready the environment, tools, materials, or workspace for delivering the service. This includes gathering supplies, setting up equipment, preparing the area, or arranging items. Typical phrases include “setting up the table”, “getting the mower ready”, “preparing the exam room”, “pulling your chart”, “setting out the groceries”, or “gathering supplies”.

### 4.0 core_service_delivery

Performance of the primary, defining task or interaction that constitutes the main value of the service. This is the central recurring activity. Typical language includes “taking your order”, “mowing the lawn”, “performing the physical exam”, “bagging your groceries”, “cleaning the kitchen”, or “checking your vitals”.

### 5.0 progress_check

Brief verification or touchpoint during the service to ensure things are proceeding as expected, gather feedback, or offer adjustments. Typical phrases include “how is everything”, “tasting good”, “everything okay so far”, “need anything adjusted”, “feeling alright”, or “looking good”.

### 6.0 additional_support

Any supplementary or value-added actions beyond the core service, such as extras, recommendations, minor enhancements, or handling small requests. Typical language includes “more water”, “side of fries”, “extra edging”, “refill your prescription”, “suggest a product”, or “add this on”.

### 7.0 completion_verification

Final check that the core service and any additions have been completed correctly and meet expectations. This includes visual inspection, testing, or confirmation of satisfaction. Typical phrases include “all finished”, “everything looks good”, “meal complete”, “exam done”, “lawn even”, or “all set”.

### 8.0 wrap_up_summary

Brief recap or communication to the customer about what was done, any observations, next steps, or follow-up information. Typical language includes “here’s what we did”, “everything taken care of”, “your checkup looks normal”, “lawn is done”, “your receipt”, or “see you next time”.

### 9.0 payment_presentation

Presentation of the cost, bill, or confirmation of any charges (if applicable; may be zero for some routine visits). This includes handing the check, showing the total, or noting auto-pay. Typical phrases include “here’s your check”, “your total is”, “no charge today”, “billing complete”, “payment due”, or “receipt ready”.

### 10.0 transaction_closure

Handling or confirmation of payment, gratuity, or financial wrap-up (if relevant). For free/recurring services, this may be simple acknowledgment. Typical language includes “paid in full”, “thank you for the tip”, “auto-pay processed”, “all settled”, “transaction complete”, or “thank you”.

### 11.0 farewell_departure

Final goodbye, thanks, invitation to return, and departure of the service provider or customer. Typical phrases include “thanks for coming”, “enjoy the rest of your day”, “see you next week”, “take care”, “come back soon”, or “safe travels”.

### 12.0 post_service_record

Internal documentation or logging of the service details for records, follow-up, billing, or quality purposes. Typical language includes “logged the visit”, “updated notes”, “recorded service”, “filed chart”, “service documented”, or “notes completed”.
